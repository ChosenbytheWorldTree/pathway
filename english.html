STEM Daily Planner

English (60 min/day)

9:00 AM - 10:00 AM

Theoretical Background

Grammar (sentence structure, tenses: present simple, past simple, future simple) forms the foundation of clear communication. Vocabulary acquisition (academic/technical terms like "algorithm," "circuit") is critical for TOEFL and MIT coursework. Listening comprehension enhances understanding of academic lectures.

Tasks

  • Grammar: Study tenses using Grammarly and LingQ (15 min)
  • Vocabulary: Learn 20 words/day on Quizlet, including terms from BBC Learning English (15 min)
  • Listening: Watch MIT OpenCourseWare lectures (e.g., 6.0001 Intro to CS) with subtitles (30 min)

Skills Developed

  • Grammar Mastery: Constructing correct sentences using basic tenses
  • Vocabulary Building: Recognizing and using academic/technical terms
  • Listening Comprehension: Following academic discussions
